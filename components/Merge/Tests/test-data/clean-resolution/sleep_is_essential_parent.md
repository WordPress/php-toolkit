# Sleep: Essential for Health

Sleep is a basic human need, important for physical and mental well-being. It allows our bodies and minds to rest.

## Why is Sleep Important?

* **Physical Health:** Sleep is involved in healing and repair of your heart and blood vessels. Ongoing sleep deficiency
  is linked to an increased risk of heart disease, kidney disease, high blood pressure, diabetes, and stroke.
* **Brain Function:** Sleep plays a critical role in brain function. When you're sleep-deprived, you have trouble
  concentrating, making decisions, and solving problems. Sleep helps your brain prepare for the next day. It’s forming
  new pathways to help you learn and remember information.
* **Emotional Well-being:**  Sleep and mood are closely connected. Poor sleep can lead to irritability, impatience, and
  an inability to cope with stress. Chronic sleep deprivation can increase your risk of depression and anxiety.
* **Immune System:**  Your immune system relies on sleep to stay healthy. This system defends your body against foreign
  or harmful substances. Ongoing sleep deficiency can change the way in which your immune system responds.
* **Physical Safety:**  Lack of sleep can affect your reaction time and decision-making, which can lead to accidents and
  injuries. This is particularly dangerous when driving or operating heavy machinery.

## How Much Sleep Do We Need?

The amount of sleep you need changes throughout your life. While sleep needs vary from person to person, most adults
need 7-8 hours of sleep per night. Teenagers need more sleep, usually 8-10 hours, and children need even more.

## Tips for Better Sleep

* **Maintain a consistent sleep schedule:** Go to bed and wake up around the same time every day, even on weekends.
* **Create a relaxing bedtime routine:**  Take a warm bath, read a book, or listen to calming music.
* **Make sure your bedroom is dark, quiet, and cool:**  These conditions are optimal for sleep.
* **Avoid caffeine and alcohol before bed:**  These substances can interfere with sleep.
* **Get regular exercise:**  But avoid intense workouts close to bedtime.
* **Limit screen time before bed:**  The blue light emitted from electronic devices can disrupt your sleep cycle.

If you're consistently having trouble sleeping, talk to your doctor. There may be an underlying medical condition that's
affecting your sleep.
