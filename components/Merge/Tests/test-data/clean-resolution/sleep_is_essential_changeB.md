# Sleep: Essential for Health

Sleep is a basic human need, important for physical and mental well-being. It allows our bodies and minds to rest.

## Why Sleep Matters

* **Physical Health:** Sleep helps repair the heart and blood vessels. Lack of sleep is linked to heart disease, kidney
  disease, high blood pressure, diabetes, and stroke.
* **Brain Function:** Sleep is crucial for the brain. When you don't sleep well, it's hard to concentrate and make
  decisions.
* **Emotional Well-being:** Sleep affects mood. Poor sleep can make you irritable. Long-term lack of sleep can increase
  the risk of depression.
* **Immune System:** Your immune system needs sleep.
* **Safety:** Lack of sleep can affect your reaction time, leading to accidents.

## How Much Sleep?

Most adults need 7-8 hours of sleep. Teenagers need 8-10 hours. Children need more.

## Better Sleep Tips

* **Regular Schedule:** Go to bed and wake up at the same time.
* **Relaxing Routine:** Take a bath or read before bed.
* **Dark, Quiet, Cool Room:** Make sure your bedroom is comfortable.
* **Avoid Caffeine and Alcohol:** Don't drink these before bed.
* **Exercise:** Get regular exercise, but not too close to bedtime.

If you're consistently having trouble sleeping, talk to your doctor. There may be an underlying medical condition that's
affecting your sleep.
